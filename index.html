Frequently Asked Questions

What is Obelis AI? +

Obelis AI is an AI-powered DevOps platform that lets you deploy to your own AWS account without any DevOps knowledge. Select your GitHub repo, connect AWS account, and let AI handle the rest. Deploy in under 5 minutes.

What happens to my AWS costs? +

You pay AWS directly for your infrastructure usage. Since everything deploys to your AWS account, you get full cost transparency and no vendor markup. This gives you AWS-level control at a fraction of the cost of managed platforms.

What applications are supported? +

We currently support static websites (React, Vue, Angular), Node.js, Python, and Go applications. AI automatically detects your tech stack and configures the optimal deployment strategy.

Do I need DevOps experience? +

Not at all. Obelis AI handles all the complex DevOps heavy lifting—deployment orchestration, infrastructure provisioning, monitoring setup, and optimization. You focus on building great products, AI handles the infrastructure.

How does the AI work? +

Our AI analyzes your codebase to understand your application architecture, dependencies, and requirements. It then automatically generates optimal AWS configurations, environment variables, scaling policies, and security settings tailored to your specific application.

Who is Obelis AI built for? +

Obelis AI is perfect for solo founders, indie developers, and small teams (2-10 people) who need to deploy applications but don't want to spend weeks learning DevOps. If you can build the app but deploying feels impossible, Obelis AI is for you.

Can I export my infrastructure? +

Yes. Since everything runs in your own AWS account, you have full access to all resources. You can view, modify, or export your infrastructure at any time. There's no proprietary layer—it's standard AWS services that you fully control.

What happens if I stop using Obelis? +

Your infrastructure keeps running. Since everything is in your AWS account, stopping Obelis doesn't affect your deployed applications. You can continue managing your infrastructure directly through AWS, or bring in a DevOps engineer later. No lock-in, ever.
